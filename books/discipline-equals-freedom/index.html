Book Thoughts

Discipline Equals Freedom, Jocko Willink

I am going to refer solely to the first part of the book, since this is the part about philosophy.

Thoughts

Generally speaking you can say that his philosophy is close to my personal philosophy of Will and that there are many overlapping points.

By reading this book I got a better understanding of my own philosphy regarding Will and could refine some concepts.
In one instance he made me understand that knowledge must be a part of exercising in Will.


Interesting concepts:

  • The count is zero
    • each day you start at zero, which leaves you less room for excuses and weakness
  • being aggressive / proactive
    • not meant in a people-attacking way but in a weakness-attacking way
    • attacking your weakness relentlessly and always striving / seeking out to tackle your weaknesses
  • “The truth is what makes discipline unavoidable.”
Recommendation

Although this book is almost written in a way where the focus lies on motivating the reader, I think the first part is a good read to gain a partial overview of my own principle of Will.
The second part isn't a good read.
